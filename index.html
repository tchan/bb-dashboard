🧠 Lessons Learned (The Hard Way)

Every report failed. ~24 reports across 8+ programs, 0 accepted. Optimized for quantity of surface-level findings instead of depth of exploitation.
PROVE exploitation end-to-end. "I found creds" is not enough - test them against live systems. Every "I could..." needs a matching "I confirmed this by..."
Check if behavior is by-design. Read upstream source code/docs before assuming misconfiguration. 2x P1s got triaged as P5 because behavior was intentional.
CORS without credentials = P5 every time. Same for config exposure, info disclosure without chaining, missing headers, SSL configs.
Rate conservatively. Submit as P3, let triager upgrade. A P5 hurts reputation more than a conservative P3.
Bugcrowd brute force standard = 400+ attempts. Must show 400+ failed attempts then a successful login to prove no silent rate limiting.
What actually pays: Auth bypass with demo, IDOR with real data access, RCE/SSRF with proof, business logic flaws in payment/auth flows. Not config findings.
Depth over breadth. Max 2 targets at a time. Go deep enough to find real logic bugs instead of spreading thin across 8 programs.
Do NOT use em dashes in reports. Use regular hyphens or rewrite sentences instead.
